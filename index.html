In Japan, the legal definition of rape was broadened this summer from "forced" to "intercourse without consent". This is because the previous law did not protect those who were forced into sexual intercourse without their consent and there was a string of acquittals in several cases.

According to the police, there are approximately 2,000 rapes a year in Japan. The breakdown is about 1,400 female victims and about 65 male victims. Men were not considered victims until the law was amended in 2017.

Police arrest about 1,300 people a year on rape charges, while prosecutors prosecute about 530 cases a year. It should be added that the conviction rate in Japanese criminal trials is over 99%.

Self-defense goods are one countermeasure although not necessarily effective. I used selenium to scrape "security goods and self-defense" products from the Japanese version of Amazon's website, with these results.
